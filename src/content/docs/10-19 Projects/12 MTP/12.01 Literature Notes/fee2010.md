## Key Ideas

- Fee & Holland-Minkley identify problem-solving and critical thinking as two of the most important cognitive processes needed by Computer Science students.
- They define *Problem-Based Learning (PBL)* as a learning approach that puts students in the driver's seat and has them construct their own learning. This is done through scaffolding the problem-solving process such that the students can pull from prior learning to tackle new problems. The student must be an active part of the process.
- Students do not realize that the core of any Computer Science related goal is problem-solving. Fee & Holland-Minkley state that this is a challenge when trying to implement PBL since the students tend to be more motivated by the end product and not the process or journey to get there.
- All 52 Computer Science students at Washington & Jefferson College over a five year period that took part in the final capstone project graduated. The authors view this as an indication that the PBL approach is ultimately a successful one, though they admit that the sample size is rather small and further research including interviewing past graduates could be helpful for this topic.
